<h2>About Me</h2>

<p>
I was born on July 13, 1991 in Filorken Community, Grand Kru County, Liberia to Mr. James Jeh Doe and Mrs. Regina Weah. 
I grew up in a humble village environment where life was centered around family and community activities.
</p>

<p>
As a child, I helped my grandfather on the farm, fetched water, and cleared grass around the village. 
These early experiences helped me learn responsibility, discipline, and hard work.
</p>

</section>

<section>

<h2>Education</h2>

<p>
I began my early education in Filorken Community. During this time, I often went to school barefoot, 
like many children in the village. Despite these challenges, I remained focused on my education.
</p>

<p>
Later, I moved to Maryland County where I attended John Hilary Tubman Junior High School. 
After some time, I moved to Monrovia to live with my father and continued my studies at 
Star of the Sea Catholic School in West Point where I completed my elementary education.
</p>

<p>
I later transferred to St. Michael Catholic School in New Georgia where I completed my secondary education 
and obtained my WAEC certificate.
</p>

</section>

<section>

<h2>College Education</h2>

<p>
I obtained a C Certificate in teaching from the Intensive Teacher Training Institute. 
I later attended Tubman University and transferred to the University of Liberia.
</p>

<p>
Currently, I am an Associate of Arts (AA) degree candidate in Social Studies at the 
LICOSESES Mobile Teacher Training College.
</p>

</section>

<section>

<h2>Family</h2>

<p>

and I am also a proud father of one daughter.
</p>

</section>
